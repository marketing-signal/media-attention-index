How it works

Two axes. One honest score.

A story can flood the newswire and still miss its audience. The index separates signal from noise by scoring every event on two independent measures, then rewarding only the events that earn both.

Horizontal Coverage: How loud was it?

The sheer size of the story, weighted by how far it reached into the outlets that set the agenda.

  • Article volume, log-adjusted so one mega-story doesn't drown out the rest
  • Tier 1 penetration, the share of coverage landing in agenda-setting titles

Vertical Resonance: Did it land?

Whether the coverage reached real readers, weighted by how much emotion it carried.

  • Verified readership per article, from Memo's publisher-direct data: actual reads, not estimated reach
  • Sentiment intensity, where conviction counts and routine, neutral coverage scores low

A second attention signal

Reach is who showed up. Dwell time is who stayed.

Readership counts how many people opened a story. Dwell time, the average seconds a reader spends on the page, measures how far they actually got. Two stories with identical reach can hold attention very differently.

Methodology

How the score is built.

Coverage combines total article volume with Tier 1 penetration. Volume is log-adjusted before scaling, so a single outsized story doesn't compress the differences among the rest. An event can score well for being widely covered, for landing in influential outlets, or both.

Resonance combines verified readership per article with the emotional intensity of coverage, measured as the magnitude of net sentiment regardless of direction. Strong negative coverage commands as much attention as strong positive coverage; only neutral, routine coverage scores low.

Each component is scaled from 0 to 1 relative to this event set, with the top event on a measure anchored at 1 and the lowest at 0. Scores compare events against each other, not against a fixed external benchmark, and shift as events are added or removed.

The final index is the geometric mean of Coverage and Resonance: Score = √(Coverage × Resonance) × 100, expressed on a 1–100 scale. Unlike a simple average, it demands strength on both axes. Volume alone can't carry a story if audiences never engaged, and a handful of well-read articles can't carry it without real coverage behind them.

Inside the score · the four components

Within each axis the two components are averaged equally. Coverage and Resonance then combine as a geometric mean, √(Coverage × Resonance), not a weighted sum, so a weak axis pulls the whole score down.

About the data, and its limits

This edition draws on Signal AI's earned-media dataset, which centres on U.S. coverage and Tier 1 business, financial, technology, and national news outlets. It may under-represent international, regional, trade, and niche titles. Read the results as a view of high-impact U.S. earned media, not the entire global landscape.
